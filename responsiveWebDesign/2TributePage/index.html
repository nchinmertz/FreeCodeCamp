Taylor Swift

The Music Industry

Here's a timeline of Swift's career

  • 2006 - Releases her self-titled debut album
  • 2008 - Releases her second studio album: Fearless. It goes on to be the most-awarded country album of all time going onto winning Album of the Year at the 52nd Grammy Awards. To support the album she embarks on her first headlining tour, The Fearless Tour, in 2009
  • 2010 - Releases her third studio album, Speak Now, which is entirely self-written. To support the album she embarks on The Speak Now World Tour in 2011
  • 2012 - Releases her fourth studio album, Red, which contains the some of her first pure pop songs. To support the album she embarks on The Red Tour in 2013 which goes on to become the highest-grossing country tour.
  • 2014 - Releases her fifth studio album, 1989. This marks her transition from country to pop music and becomes her best-selling album. It goes on to win Album of the Year at the 58th Grammy Awards making Swift the first woman to ever win the award twice. To support the album she embarks on The 1989 World Tour which goes on to become the highest-grossing tour of 2015.
  • 2015 - Swift threathens to take her music off of Apple Music if they do not pay artists royalties to artists during user free-trial periods. This prompted Apple to change their policy and pay artists during this period.
  • 2016 - SNAKEGATE! Swift is cancelled over the fued between her and Kanye West over the lyric "I think me and Taylor might still have sex/I made that bitch famous" in his song famous and the subsequent music video.
  • 2017 - Releases her sixth studio album, reputation, and embarks on the Reputation Stadium Tour to support it
  • 2018 - Signs record deal with Universal Music Group which includes a provision for Swift to maintian ownership of her masters.
  • 2019 - Releases her seventh studio album: Lover. During the promotion of the album, the master recordings of her previous six albums were sold to Scooter Braun with the only way for her to reclaim them was to exchange a new album for each old one. In response, she began rerecording her back catalog in 2020.
  • 2020 - Surprise-releases her eight and ninth studio albums, folklore and evermore, during the COVID-19 pandemic. folklore goes onto win Album of the Year award at the 63rd Grammy Awards making her the first woman to win the award three times.
  • 2021 - Releases the first two re-recorded albums, Fearless (Taylor's Version) and Red (Taylor's Version). Both re-recordings included cut songs from the orginal albums dubbed "From the Vault".
  • 2022 - Releases her tenth studio album, midnights. It goes on to win Album of the Year at the 67th Grammy Awards making Swift the first artist to win the award four times.
  • 2023 - Due to the volume of albums Swift had not been able to tour, she embarks on the Ears Tour, a tribute to her entire discography. It went on to become the highest-grossing tour in history and catapulted her into a new stratosphere of fame dubbed "Swiftmania". During the tour, Swift anounced two more re-recordings, Speak Now (Taylor's Version) and 1989 (Taylor's Version), both including "vault" songs.
  • 2024 - Releases her eleventh studio album: The Tortured Poets Department
  • 2025 - Purchases the masters of her first six original studio albums from Shamrock Holdings, which had acquired them from Braun in 2020. This marked the end of the re-recording project, leaving reputation and her self-titled albums untouched. Moving on from the project, she announces her twelfth studio album: The Life of a Showgirl.
